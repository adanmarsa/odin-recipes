Biriyani Recipe

Delicious Plate of Biriyani

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice
  • Chicken pieces (or lamb, beef, or vegetables)
  • Onions (thinly sliced)
  • Tomatoes (chopped)
  • Yogurt
  • Garlic paste
  • Ginger paste
  • Green chilies
  • Cooking oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Lemon juice
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon stick, cumin seeds)
  • Biryani masala or spice mix (can include turmeric, coriander powder, chili powder, etc.)
  • Saffron (soaked in warm milk)
  • Salt
  • Water

Steps

  1. Rinse the basmati rice and soak it in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil, add salt and whole spices, then cook the rice until it’s about 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate pot or deep pan, heat oil or ghee and fry sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing later.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste to the remaining onions and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  5. Add chicken pieces and cook until they turn white and start to brown slightly.
  6. Stir in chopped tomatoes, yogurt, green chilies, and biryani masala. Cook until the chicken is well coated and the oil begins to separate.
  7. Add chopped coriander and mint leaves, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Mix well.
  8. Layer half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture.
  9. Sprinkle some of the fried onions, coriander, mint, and a few drops of saffron milk over the rice.
  10. Add the remaining rice on top and repeat the garnish layer.
  11. Cover tightly (you can seal the lid edges with dough or use a heavy lid) and cook on low heat for 20–25 minutes to allow the flavors to blend (“dum” method).
  12. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix layers slightly without breaking the rice.
  13. Serve hot with raita, salad, or chutney.
